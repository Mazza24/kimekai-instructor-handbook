1Welcome & purpose

Read this in full during onboarding, then keep it close.

Welcome to the Kimekai team. As an instructor you are the face of Kimekai Highett and the single biggest influence on whether a student stays, improves and recommends us to others. This handbook sets out what we expect, how we work, and how you will be developed and assessed, so every instructor delivers the same high standard.

Purpose

At Kimekai, instructors do more than teach karate. We develop confidence, character, resilience, leadership and community. Every class, conversation and interaction should leave students feeling welcomed, valued, challenged and supported.

The Kimekai instructor mission

Success is not measured by what we teach. Success is measured by the impact we have on our students.

Students may join because of karate. They stay because they feel recognised, connected and part of something meaningful.

4Class delivery & teaching excellence

Safe, structured classes

  • Deliver every class in a safe, structured and organised way.
  • Know and follow the Kimekai curriculum for the cohort you are teaching.
  • Manage class energy so students stay engaged from start to finish.
  • Continue learning and improving your own technical knowledge.

Positive feedback standard

Give specific, genuine encouragement in every class. Good is at least 5 positive feedback points per class; great is 10 or more.

Know every student

  • Learn and use every student's name.
  • Recognise effort and improvement, not just ability.
  • Good instructors know most students; great instructors know every student.

5Student experience & retention

Students join because of karate and stay because of how we make them feel. Retention is everyone’s responsibility.

Welcome new students

  • Personally welcome every new student and help them feel part of the group quickly.
  • Good instructors welcome most new students; great instructors welcome every new student.

Recognise and re-engage

  • Recognise effort and improvement in class.
  • Notice absences. Good instructors notice when a student is away; great instructors re-engage students who have stopped attending.

Parent engagement

  • Build rapport with parents. Good is regular interaction; great is engaging parents every class.
  • Parents trust good instructors, and recommend Kimekai because of great ones.

Promote opportunities

  • Promote events, gradings and competitions. Good is promoting regularly; great is using every opportunity.

The Kimekai standard

Leave the dojo in a condition that lets the next staff member walk in and begin work immediately, without needing to clean, organise or rectify anything. Leave it better than you found it. Every member should walk into a clean, professional and welcoming environment.

10The Kimekai non-negotiables

Every instructor, every shift, without exception.

  1. Be prepared and punctual.
  2. Learn and use student names.
  3. Give positive feedback every class.
  4. Create a safe and enjoyable environment.
  5. Continue improving.
  6. Support fellow instructors.
  7. Promote club opportunities.
  8. Help students succeed.
  9. Protect the Kimekai culture.
  10. Leave every student better than when they arrived.

11Our philosophy

The greatest instructors are remembered not for the techniques they taught, but for the positive impact they had on the lives of others.

That responsibility should guide everything we do.
